Houm FAQ's

What is Houm?

Houm is a truly secure and private place on the internet that you own. It’s your digital home, similar to the home you have in the physical world. As is the case with your home in the real world, Houm is where you keep your digital belongings and do stuff the way you want without worrying about someone seeing or analysing you.

Why Houm?

Today, you don’t own anything on the Internet! You visit various websites or internet products for their services, create accounts, put your information and digital content there, network or communicate with each other, etc. It might seem like you own these accounts and it’s all private, but actually no. You are merely getting access to these applications that let you use their products and facilities, and in the process, you put all your data there including your photos, documents, emails, chats, etc. You don’t own your digital belongings that you put there – because you don’t have any ownership over the servers or domains where you put your data, nor do you have any control over them. With all your data lying around in different platforms and products and storage spaces not owned by you, you now have to constantly worry about your personal data getting leaked to the world. Houm is your private place on the internet that you completely own, and can be accessed only by you from anywhere in the world.

 How can I own a private place on the internet?

There’s no magic. It’s quite simple. The Internet is made up of millions of servers connected to each other. These servers host data and applications that we access and use every day. The owners of these servers can decide who can access and who cannot. The owners set the rules. So, for you to own a private place on the internet, you need to own and control a server that is connected to the rest. To access the server from anywhere easily, you need to buy a domain and map it to the server. For example, 1) you buy the domain www.myname.com and 2) map it to a server that you own and control, are the only two steps you need to take, to own a private place on the internet. In simple terms, Houm has automated these two steps for you, since not everyone knows how to do them. Anyone can type www.myname.com in the browser URL field and reach your server. You can decide who can access and who cannot, because, here you are the owner.

 How can I buy my own domain?

The working of the Internet is coordinated by a body called ICANN[1]. There are ICANN-accredited entities called Domain Registrars who are authorized to Sell Domain Names. You can buy an available domain name from an ICANN accredited Domain Registrar or an authorised reseller. Houm Technology Singapore Pte. Ltd. owns an ICANN-accredited Domain Registrar from whom you buy a domain name when you build your Houm. Hence, you have legal ownership of your domain. Houm does not own your domain name, but you do. You can buy a domain name for up to 10 years. The price of each domain depends on that specific domain name and there is no fixed standard rate for a domain name.

 OK. How do I get a server?

Once you have a domain name, you need to map it to a server that you own and control. That’s the second and final step that Houm automates for you. Houm starts up a fresh server, maps the server IP to your domain name, installs the required applications and security software in your server and hands over the ownership and control of that server to you. Hence we say, you own your digital Houm.

 But I don’t see any server? Where’s my server?

Today, there are two main ways you can own a server: 1) Buy a physical server from your neighbourhood computer store and take it home, or 2) buy a virtual server online from any large data center. The server we keep at home gives us a higher sense of security and control. However, that is naïve. The server at home, once connected to the internet is far more vulnerable to online attacks. It is also exposed to possible hazards such as theft, fire, accidental damage or intermittent power fluctuations that can damage the server. On the contrary, the server you buy online from any large and reputed data centre such as Amazon Web Services, Google Cloud Platform or DigitalOcean is significantly more secure. From basic security features such as temperature control, uninterrupted power supply, and 24X7 physical security guards, they also provide additional security features such as industry-grade firewall, automatic backup facility, seismic zone safeguards, etc. These standards and security measures make buying a server online much more secure, even though you don’t see the server physically.

 But, if my server is in a far-away Data Centre, won't they access my data, and I lose Privacy?

No. Once you have bought a server online, you can password protect it, so that no one can access the data in it. Additionally, you can install file-level and server-level 256-bit encryption of all data in your server, so that even if the server is physically compromised, the data is not accessible to anyone including the data center folks.

 So, what does Houm do for me?

Houm automates the following steps for you: 1. It helps you buy a domain in your personal name and own it. 2. Ups a server exclusively for you. 3. Maps your domain name to your server’s IP number, and publishes DNS, so that you can access your Houm over the internet. 4. Installs our product application into your server to help you manage your documents, photos, chat with other Houm owners privately, etc., essentially converting your server into your digital home you own on the internet. 5. Applies security measures such as SSL, Encryption, etc.

 Can Houm employees or engineers access my data?

Houm’s technology is architectured such that, once the above steps are completed, even employees and engineers of Houm cannot access or see the data you store in your Houm. No one can access your Houm but you.

 What is Neighbourhood?

Neighbourhood is a private group of people connected to each other through their respective Houms. Once you have a Houm, you can create multiple Neighbourhoods. After creating a Neighbourhood, you can chat with the members of that neighbourhood or share files or pictures from the security of your Houm, and all the members in that Neighbourhood will have received it. It goes from your Houm directly to their Houm without anyone intercepting it. You can create multiple neighbourhoods depending on the group of people you want in each of your neighbourhood.

How can I build a neighbourhood?

In the Neighbourhood section of your Houm, you can enter the email id or Houm id of the person whom you want to invite to a specific neighbourhood. Once they accept your invitation, they are added to that neighbourhood. You can create multiple neighbourhoods depending on the group of people you want in each of your neighbourhood.

 What happens if I don’t renew my Houm?

“With great power comes great responsibility”. You own your Houm and it’s your responsibility to make sure you renew the domain and hosting space on time. We’ll notify you multiple times when it is up for renewal, but we can’t do much after that. The same applies to auto-renewal as well. We’ll notify you if your payment method is failing. In case you fail to renew on time, we will decommission the server and your Houm won’t be live after that. We’ll send multiple emails and urge you to renew or download the entire data before the server is decommissioned. Once the server is decommissioned, you’ll lose your Houm and the data will not be retrievable. We don’t keep a copy of your data. So, data lost is permanently lost.

 Why do I see so many address types in my domain settings?

Buying and owning a domain is a serious business. You are the legal owner of that domain. It is mandated by ICANN to give registrant, administrative, technical and billing contact details for a domain owner at the time of registration of a domain. You can also update them from the ‘Domain’ section inside the Config.

 What is Whois information?

ICANN mandates to know who is responsible for a domain name. The contact details of the owner are shown against a domain in case someone wants to reach out to the owner. Houm protects your privacy by making it a default feature to mask your contact information.

 What is the need for SSL?
